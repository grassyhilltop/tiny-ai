Goldilocks Dosage

A tiny neural network with a real job: is the dose too low, too high, or just right? You are the optimizer. Turn the knobs.

The problem: a medicine works only in a middle range. Too little does nothing; too much is harmful. Our data: dose 0.0 was 0% effective, dose 0.5 was 100% effective, dose 1.0 was 0% effective. Can two ReLU neurons learn this bump? (A single straight line never could; this is why networks need nonlinearity.)

Loss: -

Hover any knob to light up where that weight lives in the equation. Each hidden neuron is a "hinge" (ReLU); the network bends two hinges into a bump. When you have felt how hard hand-tuning is, press the backprop button and watch the automatic hand do exactly what you were doing, but with calculus.
